Shakira and Burna Boy Just Released "Dai Dai" and It Is Already One of the Most Watched Videos on YouTube
May 23 2026

Shakira and Burna Boy have released their joint single "Dai Dai" along with an official music video, and the internet responded immediately. The video crossed 5 million views on YouTube within its first day, placing it at the top of the trending charts and confirming what both artists' fanbases already knew: this collaboration was going to land.

The pairing makes sense on paper and delivers on it in practice. Shakira has spent the past two years rebuilding her commercial presence with a string of releases that skew toward Afrobeats and Latin fusion, leaning into rhythmic complexity rather than pure pop accessibility. Burna Boy is currently one of the most commercially potent artists in the world, with a sound that translates across markets in a way few African artists have managed historically. Putting them together is not a stunt, it is a calculated cultural bridge.

"Dai Dai" sits in that specific zone where a song works at 7am in a car and also at midnight in a crowded room. The production gives Burna Boy the space to do what he does while building a track that still feels like a Shakira record. The video matches the energy, shot with the kind of visual scale that signals both artists were fully invested.

The release arrives at a moment when the global music market is genuinely rewarding cross-cultural collaboration rather than just tolerating it. If the streaming numbers match the YouTube momentum, this one is going to be in rotation for a while.
