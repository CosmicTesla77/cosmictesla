Marathon Season 2 Is Bungie's Last Real Shot at Saving Its Troubled Extraction Shooter
June 03 2026

Marathon is back near the top of the Twitch and Steam charts, and the timing is not a coincidence. Bungie just launched Season 2, dubbed Nightfall, on June 2, and paired it with the studio's first ever full free week running June 2 through June 9. After everything this game has been through, that free week feels less like a celebration and more like a lifeline.

## What Has Marathon Been Through?

To understand the stakes, you have to understand the road that got here. Marathon was originally slated for September 2025 before being delayed indefinitely. Along the way it picked up a wave of bad press: a playtest that struggled to crack 10,000 concurrent players on Steam, accusations that the art team had lifted designs from an independent artist, and reports that Sony had quietly paused the marketing campaign. The game finally shipped on March 5, 2026, at $40, a deliberately lower price point that read as an acknowledgment of how skeptical the audience had become.

## What Is Marathon Actually Like to Play?

The game itself is a Y2K flavored science fiction extraction shooter set on the planet Tau Ceti, where you play a runner dropping into hostile zones to grab loot and extract alive against both AI enemies and other players. The production values are real. Bungie assembled a stacked voice cast that includes Roger Clark from Red Dead Redemption 2, Jennifer English and Neil Newbon from Baldur's Gate 3, and Ben Starr from Clair Obscur. The gunplay and level design have earned consistent praise. The bones are good.

## What Is the Real Problem Facing Marathon?

The problem has never been quality. It has been relevance. Reviews landed in positive territory, but sales reportedly came in well below Sony's internal expectations. One review bluntly warned that the game risks being forgotten before the end of 2026 unless Bungie makes the story more prominent and keeps the content flowing. The extraction shooter genre is brutally competitive, and a game that is merely good in a crowded field tends to bleed players to whatever launched last.

## Why Does Season 2 Matter So Much?

Season 2 is where Bungie is making its stand. Offering a complete free week, rather than the usual weekend trial, signals that the studio knows the install base needs to grow and is willing to give the game away to do it. The wrinkle is timing. Bungie's own Destiny 2 is dropping a major update on June 9, and a large slice of Marathon's existing playerbase migrated over from Destiny in the first place. Asking those players to split their attention during the exact same window is a strange bit of internal competition. For what it is worth, PC players currently outnumber console players roughly two to one, so the Steam concurrent numbers over the next week are the metric that actually matters.

## Can Bungie Still Turn It Around?

My take is that Bungie has earned a little patience and is rapidly running out of runway to use it. The studio has publicly committed to a roadmap through Season 5, about a year out, which means they are not giving up. But live service games live and die on momentum, and a free week is a one time tool you cannot keep pulling. If the Nightfall launch and the open door do not meaningfully grow the playerbase, the conversation shifts from "how does Bungie fix this" to "how long does Sony keep funding it." Watch the next seven days closely.
