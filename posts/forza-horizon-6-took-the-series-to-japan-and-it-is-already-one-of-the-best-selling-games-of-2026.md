Forza Horizon 6 Took the Series to Japan and It Is Already One of the Best-Selling Games of 2026
June 05 2026

Forza Horizon 6 released on May 19, 2026 for Xbox Series X and S, PC, Steam, and Xbox Cloud Gaming, with a PlayStation 5 version planned for later in the year. Developed by Playground Games with assistance from Turn 10 Studios and published by Microsoft, the game takes the Horizon Festival to Japan for the first time, with a roster of over 550 cars and what the studio describes as the largest map in franchise history.

## Why Did Japan Take So Long to Happen?

Japan has been the single most requested Forza Horizon setting for over a decade, and the answer for why it took until the sixth mainline entry comes down to licensing and authenticity. Japanese automakers have historically been conservative with their intellectual property in racing games, and getting the depth of roster Playground wanted required agreements that took time to secure. The result is a car list that covers Japanese domestic market legends alongside global supercars, with models that have never appeared in a Forza title before. The map covers urban environments, mountain touge roads, coastal highways, and the iconic shadow of Mount Fuji in a way that the reveal trailer leaned into heavily. The game was announced at the Tokyo Game Show in September 2025 and launched roughly eight months later.

## How Has the Game Performed at Launch?

The numbers are significant. Forza Horizon 6 had reportedly sold 500,000 copies on Steam alone before it even launched, based on pre-order data. At launch it hit over 300,000 concurrent players on Steam, immediately beating the all-time record set by its predecessor. Total player count across platforms crossed one million in its first days, and the game has now sold 4.9 million copies despite Xbox Game Pass offering it to subscribers at no additional cost on launch day. That Game Pass figure is worth dwelling on because it represents a real tension in Microsoft's strategy: the game is a commercial success by traditional metrics, but Microsoft itself has acknowledged that the subscription model competes with direct sales of its own titles. Playground Games meanwhile confirmed that the Steam Deck version is Verified, meaning it runs natively optimized on Valve's handheld.

## What Makes Forza Horizon 6 Worth Playing Right Now?

The series has always excelled at making driving feel accessible and cinematic simultaneously, and the Japan setting gives that formula new visual material to work with. The progression systems, weekly Festival Playlist challenges, and continuous content updates that have defined the Horizon model since Forza Horizon 3 are all present. The game includes accessibility options including a Granular High Contrast Mode, a Car Proximity Radar, and an AutoDrive feature that lets players experience the world and music without manual input. Two premium expansions are planned post-launch. At $69.99 for the standard edition or included with Game Pass Ultimate, Forza Horizon 6 is the easiest entry point into the franchise since the series began.
