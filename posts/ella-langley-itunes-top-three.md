Ella Langley Just Did Something Incredibly Rare on the iTunes Charts
May 24 2026
Ella Langley is currently holding the number one, number two, and number three spots on the US iTunes Top Songs chart simultaneously, and it is worth stopping to appreciate exactly how absurd that is. In a music landscape fragmented across streaming platforms, social media algorithms, and a thousand competing artists fighting for seconds of attention, occupying the entire podium on the most visible real-time sales chart in the country is not a thing that just happens. It is the kind of statistical anomaly that gets talked about for years.
The three songs doing the work are Choosin Texas at number one, Be Her at number two, and I Cant Love You Anymore at number three, the last of which she shares with Morgan Wallen. The fact that a collaboration with one of country musics biggest names is sitting third while her solo cuts dominate above it tells you everything about where Ella Langley stands right now. She is not riding someone elses momentum. She is generating her own.
Langley has been building quietly for a few years inside the country world, but 2025 and early 2026 accelerated everything. Her writing style leans into unfiltered emotional honesty without the polished sentimentality that can make mainstream country feel sanitized. Choosin Texas in particular hits a specific nerve, that feeling of consciously choosing a place and a life even when easier options exist. It does not preach. It just plants its feet.
What makes this chart sweep stranger is the timing. She does not have a new album release driving this. There is no major label push happening in real time. What you are watching is cumulative word of mouth and streaming crossover arriving at a single chokepoint on the charts. That almost never happens this cleanly.
The Morgan Wallen collab slot is also worth examining strategically. Wallen has spent years being one of the most reliable chart performers in any genre, and yet here he is providing a feature on someone elses song while her solo work outranks it. That is a signal to the industry about where the center of gravity in that collaboration actually sits.
Whether Langley can convert this moment into the kind of sustained mainstream career that outlasts a few viral chart positions is the real question. History is full of artists who swept a moment and then watched the moment move on. But right now, on May 24 2026, she owns the top three spots, and that is a fact that does not need any qualification attached to it.
