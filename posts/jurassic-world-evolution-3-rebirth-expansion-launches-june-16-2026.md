Jurassic World Evolution 3 Rebirth Expansion Launches June 16 2026
June 16 2026

The Jurassic World Evolution 3 Rebirth Expansion launches today, June 16 2026, on PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X and S for $24.99. It is the biggest paid add on yet for Frontier Developments dinosaur park simulator, set on a research island called Saint Hubert in the period before the events of the Jurassic World Rebirth film. The pack adds four new species headlined by the hybrid Distortus rex, three top secret research sites, and a power management system the series has never had before. The base game is required, and the expansion arrives the same day across all three platforms with no staggered rollout.

## What is in the Rebirth Expansion?

The headline is the Distortus rex, the deformed hybrid creature pulled straight from the Jurassic World Rebirth movie, joined by three more new species and a fresh narrative campaign across the research sites. The new power mechanic forces managers to think about energy distribution in a way earlier games never demanded, which reshapes how you lay out a park rather than just decorating it. Frontier has confirmed that additional content is planned throughout its next fiscal year, which runs until the end of March 2027, so this is not the final chapter.

Frontier has also kept the free updates flowing, a habit that earned goodwill across the two earlier games. Species from Jurassic World Evolution 2 have been returning at no cost in monthly family units since the start of 2026, and recent updates folded in creatures like the Majungasaurus, the Dracorex, and the Liopleurodon. That steady cadence of free additions sitting alongside the paid expansion is part of why the player base has stayed engaged eight months after launch.

## How does it connect to the Jurassic World Rebirth movie?

Jurassic World Rebirth, the seventh film in the franchise, arrived in July 2025 with a grim premise: most of the dinosaurs that escaped in earlier movies are dying off, surviving only in a few remote equatorial pockets. Frontier wisely ignored that downbeat setup for the core game, letting players build thriving parks everywhere from North America to Japan, Italy, China, and Indonesia. The Rebirth Expansion is where the studio leans into the film directly, placing you on Saint Hubert before the movie's events and handing you the secret science behind its creatures.

The connective tissue runs deeper than a single dinosaur. Jeff Goldblum returns once again as Ian Malcolm, the only character voiced by his original film actor, while familiar faces like Cabot Finch chatter over the radio handing out objectives. That continuity is a big part of the appeal for fans who have followed the simulation series since 2018.

## Is the base game still worth getting in 2026?

Jurassic World Evolution 3 released on October 21 2025 and built on its predecessors with juvenile dinosaurs, more than 80 species, and over 70 creatures that can breed and raise their own family units with distinct male, female, and juvenile forms. Park guests react to the animals, pterosaurs land and walk, and semi aquatic species head into the water to feed, giving the parks a sense of life that earlier entries lacked. For anyone who enjoys management sims or simply loves dinosaurs, it remains an easy recommendation.

The expansion arriving today makes the timing good. New buyers get a polished base game with months of free content already added, plus a meaty paid chapter that ties directly into the newest film. If the genre appeals to you at all, this is a strong moment to jump in rather than wait.
