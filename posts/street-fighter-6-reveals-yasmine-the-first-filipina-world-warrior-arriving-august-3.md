Street Fighter 6 Reveals Yasmine the First Filipina World Warrior Arriving August 3
June 18 2026

Street Fighter 6 revealed full gameplay for Yasmine, a new Year 4 downloadable fighter who releases on August 3 2026 across PlayStation 5, PlayStation 4, Xbox Series X and S, PC, and Nintendo Switch 2. Yasmine is a high schooler from the Philippines who fights with a crescent shaped karambit blade, and she stands as the first Filipina world warrior in the nearly forty year history of the series.

Capcom first teased Yasmine as part of the Year 4 lineup during Summer Game Fest 2026 before pulling back the curtain on her moveset in a dedicated gameplay trailer. She joins a roster that has steadily grown more international and more stylistically varied, and her arrival is being celebrated loudly by a Filipino fighting game community that has waited a long time to see itself represented at the top tier of the genre.

## What is Yasmine's fighting style?

Yasmine fights using Arnis and Eskrima, the Filipino weapon based martial arts, wielding her karambit knife for rapid close range strikes. Her kicks draw on Yaw Yan, a discipline nicknamed the Dance of Death, and she layers in wind based properties similar to the veteran character Rashid, letting her fire off projectiles and control space at a distance.

That blend of a bladed rushdown game and ranged wind tools makes her one of the more mechanically distinct newcomers in some time. Capcom has framed Yasmine and the rest of the Season 4 arrivals as characters built for new and younger players rather than longtime veterans, which suggests an accessible execution barrier paired with flashy, expressive tools that look good in motion.

## Why does Yasmine matter beyond the roster?

Yasmine matters because representation in a series this old and this globally popular carries real weight. Street Fighter has always leaned on national archetypes, and adding a Filipina fighter who draws directly from authentic Filipino martial arts is a meaningful first for a community that has produced world class competitors for years without a character to call its own.

There is a story hook too. Yasmine is searching for her long lost brother, and despite a shy and gentle demeanor she turns fierce the moment her blade comes out, a contrast that gives her more personality than a typical guest slot. Between the cultural milestone and a kit that genuinely looks fresh, she is shaping up to be one of the more anticipated additions in the game's post launch life.
