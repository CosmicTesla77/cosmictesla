# Forza Horizon 6 Is Dominating Steam as the Number One Top Seller Right Now
2026-05-21

Forza Horizon 6 has landed on Steam and it immediately claimed the number one top seller spot, priced at $69.99 and pulling in a Very Positive rating from early reviewers. For fans of open-world racing games, this is the release they have been waiting for, and the early response suggests Microsoft and Playground Games have delivered something worth the price of entry.

The Forza Horizon series has built its reputation on one core promise: give players a massive, beautiful open world and let them drive anything they want through it at full speed. Each installment has moved to a new location, and the franchise has used that format to showcase destinations ranging from the British countryside to Mexico to Australia. Horizon 6 continues that tradition, and early coverage points to a world that raises the visual and environmental bar again.

What separates Horizon from simulation-focused racing titles is its accessibility. You do not need to memorize braking points or tune suspension setups to have a great time. The game hands you a car, opens the world, and lets you set the pace. That approach has made it one of the most successful racing franchises in gaming history, crossing demographics that typically do not overlap in the genre.

At $69.99, this sits at the premium tier for PC gaming, but the Forza Horizon series has historically justified that price with content volume. Hundreds of cars, dozens of seasonal events, and a world designed to be explored for hundreds of hours make the math work for most players.

If you are a racing game fan or just want an excuse to drive a Lamborghini through a redwood forest at 180 miles per hour with no consequences, Forza Horizon 6 is available now on Steam and is already earning its top seller position.
