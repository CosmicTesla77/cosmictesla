Strangers by Belle Burden Tops the NYT List and Lands a Netflix Deal
June 30 2026

Strangers: A Memoir of Marriage by Belle Burden debuted at number one on the New York Times bestseller list and held the top spot for seven weeks, and Netflix won a bidding war for the screen rights with Gwyneth Paltrow attached to star and executive produce. The memoir traces the sudden collapse of Burden's roughly two decade marriage during the first weeks of the COVID pandemic, and it has struck a nerve far beyond its rarefied Manhattan and Martha's Vineyard setting.

## What Is Strangers About?

The book opens in March 2020 with Burden and her family hunkered down at their Martha's Vineyard home, building fires and making roast chicken in the early uncertainty of lockdown. Then a stranger sends her a message claiming his wife is having an affair with her husband, a prominent hedge fund manager she calls James. He admits the affair, separates from the family, and states he does not want custody of their children. Burden walks the reader through the divorce and its psychological aftermath, including her earlier decision to stop working and hand financial control of their shared life to her husband.

## Why Did the Memoir Resonate So Widely?

The New York Times Editors' Choice review described the book as reading like a love story, a horror story, and, in one tense stretch, a financial thriller, which captures why it traveled so far. Burden reported receiving more than a thousand messages from women, and some men, who saw their own marriages in hers. Critics did debate the privilege baked into the story, noting that Burden's inherited wealth meant her long term security was never truly at risk, yet reviewers still praised the restraint and clarity of her storytelling. The deeper question the book poses, whether anyone can truly know the person they share a life with, is what gives it reach beyond its zip code.

## How Did Belle Burden Turn a Column Into a Bestseller?

The memoir grew out of a 2023 New York Times Modern Love column titled Was I Married to a Stranger, which announced the end of her marriage to a wide audience and tested her writing in public for the first time. Burden, the daughter of Amanda Burden and Carter Burden and a trained lawyer from an old money family, expanded that column into a full length account published by Penguin Random House. The book reached a fifth printing and spent several months on the bestseller list, and the Netflix adaptation with Paltrow cements its move from personal essay to cultural moment.

There is a hard, practical lesson under the heartbreak. Burden writes candidly about ceding financial oversight to her spouse and the exposure that created, a warning that applies to anyone who lets a partner control the money without staying informed.

As a portrait of how the person closest to you can become a stranger overnight, the memoir earns its place at the top of the list.
