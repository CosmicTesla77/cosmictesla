Gears of War: E-Day Lands October 6 2026 as an Xbox and PC Exclusive
June 07 2026

Gears of War: E-Day releases October 6 2026 as an Xbox Series X/S and PC exclusive, and it arrives on Xbox Game Pass on day one. Developer The Coalition closed the 2026 Xbox Games Showcase with the first real gameplay footage, the first time a Gears game has ever anchored the show.

## What is Gears of War E-Day about?

E-Day is a prequel set fourteen years before the original 2006 Gears of War. You play as a younger Marcus Fenix alongside Dom Santiago in the city of Kalona on the planet Sera, on the exact day the Locust Horde first erupts from underground and overruns the surface world. The studio frames it as two origin stories at once: the start of the Locust War and the birth of the brotherhood between Marcus and Dom. The structural rule the team set for itself is that the camera never leaves Bravo Squad for the entire campaign.

The reveal trailer opened with Marcus in plain civilian clothing before the world falls apart, then ended with him meeting Dom for what looks like the first time. It is a deliberate contrast to the armored super soldiers fans know, and it sets up the descent into the war that defines the series.

## When does Gears of War E-Day release and where can you play it?

The release date is October 6 2026 on Xbox Series X/S and PC, with day one availability on Xbox Game Pass. Despite long running rumors of a PlayStation 5 version, Microsoft confirmed E-Day as a console exclusive to Xbox. Preorders unlock access to an open beta beginning August 6, so players get hands on time roughly two months before launch.

This is also the first Gears built entirely in Unreal Engine 5. The team rebuilt every asset, animation, enemy, and weapon from scratch rather than porting anything forward, and added a jump button and expanded movement so you can navigate the crumbling environments of Sera more freely.

## Why does E-Day matter for the franchise?

Going backward instead of forward was a bet. The Coalition chose an origin story over a straight sequel, and the early response suggests it paid off. After roughly a decade of constant production, the studio treated the engine jump as a clean slate, which is rare for a franchise this established.

For Xbox, E-Day leading the showcase signals where the company is placing its first party muscle this year. The combination of a beloved name, a fresh technical foundation, and a day one Game Pass drop makes this one of the most important Xbox releases of 2026.
